1. Introduction

This privacy policy describes how [YOUR SERVICE NAME] ("we", "us") processes personal data when you use our video conferencing platform. The platform is based on edumeet, an open-source WebRTC video conferencing solution.

We are committed to processing your data only to the extent necessary to provide the service, in accordance with Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR).

2. No Cookies

This service does not use cookies of any kind — neither essential, analytical, nor marketing cookies. Your browser will not receive any cookie from this service.

User preferences (such as selected audio/video device and language) are stored exclusively in your browser's localStorage, which never leaves your device and is not accessible to us or any third party.

4. WebRTC Media Transport

Audio and video are transmitted using WebRTC (Web Real-Time Communication), an open standard built into modern browsers. Media flows through our SFU (Selective Forwarding Unit) server, which forwards encrypted media streams between participants without decrypting or storing them.

For NAT traversal (connecting participants behind firewalls or routers), the service may use TURN relay servers. In that case your media traffic is relayed through the TURN server in encrypted form. The TURN server(s) used are: [LIST YOUR TURN SERVER(S) OR STATE "our own infrastructure"].

All signaling (session negotiation) is transmitted over an encrypted WebSocket (WSS) connection using TLS.

5. Local Storage (Your Device Only)

The following data is saved in your browser's localStorage under the key edumeetRoot. This data never leaves your device and is not accessible to us.

  • Display name (so you do not have to re-enter it on each visit)
  • Selected audio input device
  • Selected audio output device
  • Selected video input device
  • Audio quality settings (noise suppression, echo cancellation, codec preferences)
  • Video quality settings (resolution, frame rate)
  • Interface language (locale)
  • UI preferences (layout, mirror view, max visible videos)

You can clear this data at any time via your browser's settings (Clear site data / Clear localStorage).

6. Local Recording (Optional Feature)

The platform provides an optional local recording feature. When used:

  • Recording is performed entirely in your browser using the browser's MediaRecorder API.
  • The recording file is saved directly to a location on your own device that you choose via a save dialog.
  • Recording data is not uploaded to our servers.
  • Other participants are notified when recording is active.
  • If you record a session, you become the data controller for that recording and are responsible for its lawful use.

7. Background Effects and AI Processing

The platform offers optional background blur and virtual background features. This processing:

  • Is performed entirely locally in your browser using WebGL and TensorFlow Lite.
  • Does not send video frames to any external server for processing.
  • Custom background images you upload are stored in your browser's local storage only.

8. Management / Administration Features (Optional)

If your operator has enabled the management system, room administrators may log in with an email address and password. In that case:

  • Email address and a JWT authentication token are processed.
  • The token is stored in localStorage under the key feathers-jwt and is used for session continuity.
  • This applies only to administrator accounts, not to regular conference participants.

9. Login via OAuth 2.0 / OpenID Connect (OIDC) (Optional)

If the operator has enabled federated login, users (typically administrators or members of an institution) can authenticate using an external identity provider (IdP) via the OAuth 2.0 / OpenID Connect protocol. This applies only when loginEnabled is set in the service configuration — it is not available to anonymous conference participants.

How it works

When you click "Login", your browser is redirected to the identity provider's authorisation endpoint (authorize_url). You authenticate there directly — your password is never sent to this service. After successful authentication, the identity provider returns an authorisation code, which the server exchanges for a token at the provider's access_url. Your profile data is then retrieved from the provider's profile_url.

Data received from the identity provider

The default requested OAuth scope is openid profile email. The following claims may be received depending on what the identity provider returns:

Token storage

After login, a JWT token is stored in your browser's localStorage under the key feathers-jwt. This token is used to authenticate subsequent requests to the management API without requiring you to log in again. It is automatically refreshed shortly before expiry. The token is removed from localStorage when you log out.
